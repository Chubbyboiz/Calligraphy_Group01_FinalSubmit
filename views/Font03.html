    • Naskh

    The Naskh font is one of the most classic and popular Arabic fonts. It has a long history and has developed over many centuries. Here is an overview of the formation and birth of the Naskh font:

    Origin: Naskh font originated in ancient times, around the 10th century AD, in the Levant region (the area including modern Syria, Lebanon, and Jordan). Initially, it was developed to copy documents and preserve important documents.

    Evolution: The Naskh font has undergone evolution and improvement during its development. Letters and symbols are optimized to ensure uniformity and beauty in writing and printing. This development helped Naskh become a standard Arabic font for writing books, newspapers, and important documents.

    Author: No specific author has been identified for the Naskh font, as it has evolved over many centuries and through the contributions of many people in Arabic culture and language. Naskh does not belong to a specific person or group of people but is instead a writing system that has become an important part of Arabic writing culture.

    The Naskh font was and still is widely used in the Arabic language and culture, and it plays an important role in representing text and documents in Arabic script in a beautiful and consistent way.
